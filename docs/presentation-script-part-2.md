## Spoken Script

"Now I will move to the deeper collaboration features.

The Rooms page is where users discover or enter classes. Public rooms are visible to the relevant users, while private rooms use a join-code flow. A teacher or room owner creates the room, shares the code, and the intended user joins through the Rooms page. Once Convex records the membership, the room becomes visible in the user's joined rooms.

Inside a room, the collaboration flow becomes active. The room page combines the post feed, comments, replies, pinned context, AI room summary, teacher tools, different feed views, and assignment submissions. This is the core working area of the product.

The assignment submission flow is intentionally scoped to the room creator. The creator acts as the admin or reviewer for that room, so student submissions go to the correct authority and stay private to the room context. That keeps the flow organized and aligned with how academic work is normally managed.

The Planner page turns deadlines into a study schedule. It is useful because it converts academic workload into something a student can act on. Instead of just showing dates, it helps the user understand what is due next and what needs attention first.

The Analytics page gives a higher-level view of the workspace. It shows deadline risk, activity trends, content distribution, and AI risk signals. This is the kind of screen a teacher or super admin can use to understand whether a room or a course is healthy.

The Knowledge Base is the grounded Q&A layer. Its purpose is not to invent answers. It should use authorized room data, cite visible evidence, and abstain when the evidence is weak. That makes the system more trustworthy in academic use.

The AI assistant is the live chat helper. It is room-aware, grounded in the workspace, and designed to fall back to deterministic behavior if the model is unavailable. In a presentation, I would explain that this keeps the assistant useful even when the live AI service is limited.

Finally, the admin tools allow super admins to review rooms, roles, moderation, and overall governance. That is what makes the platform suitable for a real academic environment instead of a simple classroom chat app.

The overall message is that UniBoard is not a single feature. It is a full academic operating environment where collaboration, deadlines, search, AI, and governance all work together."
